ABOUT

I am an undergraduate student in Electronics and Communication at NIT Jalandhar. During my internships, research projects and working on my individual projects, the nuances of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning as a domain have interested me a lot. I find it fascinating when decades-old discoveries are provided a huge amount of data and computation power, it does wonders in virtually every domain that we can think of. Machine Learning's flexibility and its untapped potential to solve real-life problems is something I admire the most.

My research interests lie in Deep Learning, Biomedical Signal and Image Processing and Application of Machine Learning in Healthcare. Previously I have worked with the Hi-Tech Robotic team on their patented technology "Novus Aware". Novus Aware is a computer vision technology model for monitoring various features of driver's behavior including eye, face, head, gaze, and other natural cues, to infer whether the driver is in a safe driving condition or not. I also interned at Schneider Electric IT Business Pvt. Ltd. and Indian Institute of remote sensing (IIRS), ISRO.

Presently I am working on, "Haptic Feedback based navigation system for visually impaired". With this project, we aim to develop a low-cost device that can sense the environment, plan the path and help the user navigate through haptic feedback.

Hybrid CNN-LSTM model for classification of multispectral satellite images 2019 IEEE International Conference on Intelligent Computing and Remote Sensing | April’19 – July’19
(In Press) In this paper we have used deep learning techniques to build a model for the pixel classification of multispectral satellite images. In this work two of the most successful principles of deep learning have been combined, namely convolution neural networks and recurrent neural networks for classification task. In this paper a hybrid CNN-LSTM architecture has been proposed. The model achieved a staggering accuracy of 94.8 percent on 6 class classification task. Moreover this work is a testament of the fact that Neural Networks could be robust classifiers for processing multispectral data, even outperforming traditional learning techniques

Emotion analysis of time series EEG signals using Merged LSTM model
5th International Conference on Data Science and Engineering (ICDSE 2019) | February '19 – July'19
(In press) In this paper we have proposed a novel methodology to classify emotions using contemporary digital signal processing techniques such as wavelet transform and statistical measures for feature extraction and dimensionality reduction. Furthermore we developed a state of the art neural architecture for the classification task. A merged LSTM model has been proposed for binary classification of emotions. The model’s applicability and accuracy has been validated using DEAP dataset which is the benchmark dataset for emotion recognition. Results on the dataset demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over existing state of the art methods.

Error estimation in trajectory of ballistic missile using Kalman Filter
2019 IEEE International Conference on Innovations in Communication, computing and instrumentation | August '18 – January'19
(In Press) This research examines the Radar related errors, studies the extent of its impact on Anti Ballistic Missile (ABM) systems and proposes a solution to minimize the errors and increase the effectiveness of the system. In this project we have used Kalman Filtering Algorithm for increasing the accuracy and reliability of intercepted missile trajectory. The results obtained are used for error estimation in the filtered radar data and theoretically predicted trajectory of the missile. This approach considerably reduces the error and achieves a high level of accuracy in target interception.
